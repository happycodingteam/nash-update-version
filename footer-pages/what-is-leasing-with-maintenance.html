What is leasing with maintenance?

1 December 2023
Business, Basics Of Business Leasing Guide, Personal, Personal Leasing Maintenance Guide

Home - Business - What is leasing with maintenance?

Maintenance lease plans offer a fixed-cost option to ensure your vehicle is maintained to the manufacturer’s recommendations during the term of the lease. It could save you time and money, for complete hassle-free motoring.

If you choose not to take out a maintenance contract with your lease vehicle, you will be responsible for all maintenance and servicing costs throughout the duration of your contract. A maintenance contract needs to be agreed before your vehicle leasing contract is live and your car or van has been delivered.

Key benefits of maintenance

What’s included in a maintenance leasing contract?

  • Routine servicing and maintenance for the duration of the contract
  • Mechanical and electrical repairs (not covered by the warranty)
  • Fair Play / Wear only Tyres (dependent on plan*)
  • Accident Management
  • Breakdown Recovery
  • Exhausts & Batteries
  • Vehicle collection & delivery
  • MOT tests (3 years+ only)
  • Roadside Assistance & Breakdown Cover • Non fair wear and tear (i.e. body/trim damage, broken glass)

What’s not included in a maintenance leasing contract?

  • Driver error or negligence
  • Misfuelling / run out of fuel
  • Fluid top-up between services (including Ad Blue, Lubricants & Oils)
  • Missed servicing
  • Missing or damaged items
  • Clutches under 75,000 miles
  • Flywheels
  • DPF and Catalytic Converters
  • Vehicle glass
